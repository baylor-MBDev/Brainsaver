DOOMTYPE

Privacy Policy

Last updated July 8, 2026

DOOMTYPE is a screen-time tool that puts a typing gate in front of apps you choose to guard. This page explains exactly what the app does and does not do with your data.

The short version: DOOMTYPE has no account, no servers, and no analytics. Everything it stores lives only on your device. Nothing is ever sent anywhere.

What the app stores

All of it stays on your device, in the app's local storage:

  • Your gate phrase(s)
  • Which apps you've chosen to guard
  • Your re-gate interval and escalation setting
  • Daily counters (opens, phrases typed, backouts) and your streak

Uninstalling the app, or clearing its storage in Android settings, permanently deletes all of this. There is no cloud backup, account, or sync — because there is nothing to sync to.

The Accessibility Service permission

DOOMTYPE uses Android's Accessibility API for one purpose: to notice when a guarded app comes to the foreground, so it can show the typing gate. Specifically, the service:

  • Only receives the package name of the app that just came to the foreground (e.g. com.instagram.android)
  • Never reads screen content, text you type elsewhere, or anything displayed by other apps — this is disabled at the configuration level, not just by policy
  • Never transmits anything over the network — the app has no server to send it to

The "display over other apps" permission

This is what lets the gate screen appear on top of a guarded app. It's used for exactly that, and nothing else.

Permissions the app does not use

DOOMTYPE does not request access to your contacts, photos, location, microphone, camera, or files. It does not use device storage permissions at all — its local data lives in the app's private storage.

Third parties

DOOMTYPE does not include analytics, crash reporting, advertising, or any other third-party SDK. No data is shared with anyone, because none leaves your device.

Children's privacy

DOOMTYPE does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children, because it does not collect information at all — everything described above stays local to the device it's installed on.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the "Last updated" date at the top of this page will change too. Meaningful changes will also be noted in the app's release notes.
